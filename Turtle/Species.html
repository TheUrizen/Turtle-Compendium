Turtle Species

This page lists a series of our organization's favorite turtle species.

Galapagos Giant Tortoise

A tortoise is a turtle too! These giant shelled beasts are infamous for their huge size and long lifespan. They eat prickl pear cactuses, fruits, grasses, leaves, and flowers.

  • Size: Will exceed 500 pounds in weight and 5 feet in length
  • Lifespan: Have been known to reach 175 years in age.
  • Location: It's no shocker they primarily inhabit the Galapagos Islands.

Alligator Snapping Turtle

These ferocious creatures will bite your finger right off if you let them. The largest freshwater turtle in the world, they have an average bite force of 209 N.

  • Size: They can reach up to 29 inches.
  • Lifespan: They typically range from 50 to 100 years old.
  • Location: All across the Americas, most commonly in the American south, in freshwater sources.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Probably the most famous of all turtles, these wanderers are known for their giant migratory patterns, and their egg-laying escapades.

  • Size: They avarage 6 feet in length, and range from 500 to 1500 pounds when mature.
  • Lifespan: In captivity, they live up to 70 years. In the wild, 50.
  • Location: Their stomping grounds are the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian oceans.
